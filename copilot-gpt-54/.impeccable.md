## Design Context

### Users

Mixed audience: recruiters, hiring managers, potential collaborators, and technical peers evaluating Lars Nyström's experience and judgment. They arrive to quickly understand who he is, what kinds of systems he builds, how he thinks about architecture, and whether he is credible for complex production work.

### Brand Personality

Pragmatic, technical, refined. The tone should feel calm, competent, and production-tested rather than flashy, startup-like, or self-promotional. The interface should evoke confidence and precision, with enough warmth to feel human rather than sterile.

### Aesthetic Direction

Dark, refined, minimal. Use a restrained palette built from deep neutral surfaces with one muted accent, strong typography, and asymmetrical editorial composition instead of generic card-grid marketing patterns. Avoid gradient text, loud visual tricks, thick accent stripes, and glossy startup aesthetics.

### Design Principles

- Lead with credibility: emphasize systems thinking, production experience, and concrete technical competence before personal flourish.
- Keep the hierarchy sharp: strong typography, disciplined spacing, and concise copy should make scanning easy for both recruiters and technical readers.
- Use restraint as polish: motion, color, and decorative elements should be subtle and purposeful, never ornamental.
- Keep content structured: all page content should come from typed data models so the site remains maintainable and easy to evolve.
- Design responsively by intent: mobile should feel composed and usable, not like a compressed desktop layout.
